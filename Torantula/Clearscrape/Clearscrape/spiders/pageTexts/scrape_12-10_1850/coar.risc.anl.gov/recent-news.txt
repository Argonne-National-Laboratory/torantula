	<div class="entry-meta">		<span class="byline"><span class="author vcard"><i class="fa fa-user"></i><a class="url fn n" href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/author/coar/" title="COAR">COAR</a></span></span>

	<div class="entry-content clearfix">
		<h2>The Current Trend</h2>
<p>Despite intense growth in the field of cybersecurity, women continue to form merely a small minority of the cybersecurity workforce.  The industry currently boasts approximately one million available jobs, and the number is expected to increase to 1.5 million available jobs by 2019.  However, women only hold about one in every 10 positions<a href="#_ftn1" name="_ftnref1">[1]</a> in the cybersecurity industry, constituting a meager 8-13 percent<a href="#_ftn2" name="_ftnref2">[2]</a> of cybersecurity professionals overall.  Figure 1 below illustrates a comparison of the amount of women in computer science-related studies versus alternative fields:</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" class="size-full wp-image-2717 aligncenter" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/2.png" alt="" width="585" height="392" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>A number of possible reasons have been suggested for the lack of women in the cybersecurity workforce.  For example, the information-security organization CREST discusses a deficiency in computer-science courses among secondary schools.<a href="#_ftn3" name="_ftnref3">[3]</a>  Without proper exposure to the field, many women remain unaware of their opportunities.  Similarly, the workforce itself has witnessed a lack of mentorship that encourages women to pursue advancement in cybersecurity.<a href="#_ftn4" name="_ftnref4">[4]</a>  Instead of encouragement, the National Cybersecurity Institute (NCI) argues that the marketing industry tends to use aggressive, more masculine terminology in relation to cybersecurity, selecting phrases such as “combat cyberthreats” or “fortify digital defenses”.<a href="#_ftn5" name="_ftnref5">[5]</a>  The entertainment industry also tends to portray men more often than women in mathematical, scientific, or technical television and film roles.<sup>3</sup>  As a result, women may pursue fields that offer a more welcoming environment. <a href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/women-in-cybersecurity/" class="more-link"><span>Read more</span></a></p>
	</div>

			<a href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/mtd-named-2017-rd-100-finalist/" title="MORE MTD Named 2017 R&#038;D 100 Finalist">MORE MTD Named 2017 R&#038;D 100 Finalist</a>
		</h2>
	</header>

	<div class="entry-meta">		<span class="byline"><span class="author vcard"><i class="fa fa-user"></i><a class="url fn n" href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/author/coar/" title="COAR">COAR</a></span></span>

		<span class="posted-on"><a href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/mtd-named-2017-rd-100-finalist/" title="2:16 pm" rel="bookmark"><i class="fa fa-calendar-o"></i> <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-09-21T14:16:07+00:00">September 21, 2017</time><time class="updated" datetime="2017-12-07T15:15:51+00:00">December 7, 2017</time></a></span></div>
	
	<div class="entry-content clearfix">
		<p><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/RD.jpg"><img loading="lazy" class="wp-image-2754 alignright" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/RD.jpg" alt="" width="298" height="283" /></a>In an <em>R&amp;D Magazine </em><a href="https://www.rdmag.com/article/2017/08/2017-r-d-100-awards-finalists-announced">press release</a> on August 9th, the R&amp;D 100 Awards Committee announced its 2017 finalists, with COAR&#8217;s patented technology, Multiple OS Rotational Environment Moving Target Defense (MORE MTD), among them.</p>
<p>2017 R&amp;D 100 is the 55th annual awards program that recognizes prestigious innovations of science or technology.  It awards 100 ideas, each of which falls into one of five categories: Analytical/Test; IT/Electrical; Mechanical Devices/Materials; Process/Prototyping; and Software/Services, and provides four Special Recognition Awards as well: Market Disruptor Services; Market Disruptor Products; Corporate Social Responsibility; and Green Tech. <a href="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/mtd-named-2017-rd-100-finalist/" class="more-link"><span>Read more</span></a></p>
