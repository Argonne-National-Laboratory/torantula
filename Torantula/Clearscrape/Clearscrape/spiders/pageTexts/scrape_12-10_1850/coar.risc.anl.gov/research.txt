	<header class="entry-header">
		<h1 class="entry-title">Cyber Research Areas of Interest</h1>	</header>

	<div class="entry-content clearfix">
		<p>Below are the key areas of interest for the Cyber Operations, Analysis, and Research Section:</p>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-667 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ashburn_corners.png" alt="" width="151" height="139" srcset="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ashburn_corners.png 151w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ashburn_corners-150x139.png 150w" sizes="(max-width: 151px) 100vw, 151px" /></p>
<h4><a href="/ashburn-va-regional-resiliency-assessment">Internet Infrastructure Analysis</a></h4>
<p>We research large Internet infrastructure issues including cloud security, physical vulnerabilities with cascading cyber consequences, routing and backbone denial of service, fiber path redundancy and data dependency analysis.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-352 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/car_hacking.jpg" alt="" width="145" height="144" /></p>
<h4>Transportation Cybersecurity</h4>
<p>We currently collaborate with <a href="http://www.transportation.anl.gov/facilities/aprf.html">Argonne&#8217;s Advanced Powertrain Research Facility</a> (APRF) to investigate automotive cyber vulnerabilities including remote attack vectors for vehicles such as wireless and vehicle-to-vehicle communications.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-378 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/more-mtd.png" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4><a href="/research/moving-target-defense/">Moving Target Defense (MTD)</a></h4>
<p>This website now runs on our patent pending <a href="more-mtd/">MORE MTD</a> technology. We have ongoing projects to implement MTD on industrial control system platforms and for C4I and military communications.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class=" researchblock alignleft wp-image-384 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/botnets.png" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4>Botnet Mitigation</h4>
<p>Our current botnet research is directed at attacking infected botnet hosts to mitigate botnets instead of attacking the command and control centers. This technique would mitigate “zombie recidivism” and reduce resources for cyber criminals.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-671 size-thumbnail" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-150x150.png" alt="" width="150" height="150" srcset="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-150x150.png 150w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-32x32.png 32w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-64x64.png 64w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-96x96.png 96w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dependencies-128x128.png 128w" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" /></p>
<h4><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/12/SIMDependency-Slick.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Dependency Visualization</a></h4>
<p>We are developing tools to enable novel visualizations for displaying dependencies and interdependencies between critical infrastructure facilities and sectors.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-668 size-thumbnail" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-150x150.png" alt="" width="150" height="150" srcset="https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-150x150.png 150w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-32x32.png 32w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-64x64.png 64w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-96x96.png 96w, https://coar.risc.anl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/MEG-128x128.png 128w" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" /></p>
<h4>Mobile Encryption Gateway (MEG)</h4>
<p>MEG is a prototype technology that strives to bring best effort email encryption to end users. MEG will make PGP setup effectively as easy as installing a cell phone app and will establish the users’ smartphone as a private key store, allowing for ubiquitous access to private keys without key distribution.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-365 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/satellites.jpg" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4>Satellite Cybersecurity</h4>
<p>We are investigating the ability to infiltrate satellite communications via cyber attacks and use those communications to eavesdrop or spoof communications or even alter the path of satellites, possibly accelerating the Kessler effect.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-367 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/social_engineering_fatigue.jpg" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4>Social Engineering</h4>
<p>We have reviewed fatigue studies to identify vulnerabilities in guards and watch floors to social engineering attacks. We are studying spear phishing attacks to determine what specific times and patterns are generally successful.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-371 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/cyber_offense.jpg" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/HackBack.jpg" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cyber Self-Defense</a></h4>
<p>We are researching both the legal implications and the effectiveness of using offensive techniques, tactics, tools and practices as a defensive strategy and/or deterrence in the cybersecurity space.</p>
</div>
<div class="research-area-list">
<p><img loading="lazy" class="researchblock alignleft wp-image-375 size-full" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/trending_events.jpg" alt="" width="145" height="145" /></p>
<h4><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/12/Cyber-Trending-Fact-Sheet.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Security Intelligence for Trending Events</a></h4>
<p>We are improving intelligence in trending events using social media analytics, geoclustering, and dynamic, interactive visualization to allow intelligence analysts to respond quickly to emerging world events.</p>
</div>
			</div>
